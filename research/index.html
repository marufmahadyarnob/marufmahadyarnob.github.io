Green Building in Bangladesh: Prospects, Challenges and Future Opportunities

A structured academic research overview focused on sustainable construction practices, current barriers, and long-term environmental development in Bangladesh.

Author Maruf Mahady Arnob
Category Civil Engineering / Sustainability
Status In Progress
01

Abstract

In this research, it focuses on the present situation and future capability of green buildings in Bangladesh. The purpose of the research is to determine how green building concepts are being implemented and to understand the benefits and challenges related to sustainable construction practices. The study of this research mainly uses secondary data that collected from journals, many research reports, and online sources. The results show that the number of green buildings in Bangladesh has been gradually increasing. Mainly in industrial and commercial sectors. However, the large-scale adoption of these buildings is still limited due to some factors like big start-up costs and lack of social awareness. The study of this research suggests that with strong policy support and increased awareness could increase green building practices. That could contribute significantly to sustainable development in Bangladesh.

Introduction

Green building is a modern construction approach that focuses on environmental sustainability. It also focuses on reducing the negative impact of a building on the natural environment. Green buildings are designed and operated by using energy, water, and materials more efficiently than conventional buildings. They also provide a healthier and more comfortable indoor environment for occupants.

Green building is important all over the world. The building sector uses a large amount of energy and also consumes a huge amount of water. Construction requires many natural resources. Traditional buildings create more pollution. They also produce more waste. Many buildings increase carbon emissions. This contributes to climate change. It also affects public health. Because of this, many countries support green construction. Governments are promoting sustainable building policies. Engineers and researchers are also giving importance to this concept. Green buildings help reduce environmental pressure. They also help save resources for the future. For this reason, green building is now globally recognized. Green building is very relevant in Bangladesh. Bangladesh is a densely populated country where the demand for buildings is increasing rapidly. Urbanization is growing every year. Industrial development is also increasing. This creates pressure on land and resources and also increases energy demand. Water demand is also rising. Traditional construction creates more environmental stress. Bangladesh already faces climate- and air pollution-related risks. In addition, unplanned urban development creates more challenges. In this situation, green building can be a useful solution. It can support sustainable urban growth and reduce resource consumption. Some progress is already visible in Bangladesh, especially in the industrial and commercial sectors. Many RMG factories have adopted green features. Some buildings have also received international recognition. However, overall adoption is still limited. That is why this topic is important in Bangladesh.

Green building is gaining attention in Bangladesh. However, its adoption is still limited. Only a small number of buildings follow green principles. Most of these are industrial or commercial buildings. Residential adoption is still very low. Many conventional buildings still follow traditional methods. This creates a gap in sustainable development. The need for green buildings is increasing. But actual implementation is still slow. There are several possible reasons behind this. High initial cost is one major barrier. Lack of public awareness is another barrier. Many developers still prefer traditional construction. Technical knowledge is also limited in some cases. Policy support is not always strong enough. Financial incentives are also limited. Because of these problems, green building is not yet common. So, this issue needs proper study. It is important to understand the current condition. It is also important to identify the barriers. Future opportunities should also be explored.

The main objective of this study is to examine green buildings in Bangladesh. It focuses on the current status of green building practices. It also focuses on future prospects. The study aims to understand how green concepts are being applied. It also tries to identify the present level of adoption. Another objective is to discuss the major benefits. These include energy efficiency and water saving. They also include environmental protection. The study also aims to identify key challenges. These challenges may be financial, technical, or social. It also aims to explore future opportunities. The research will suggest possible ways for improvement. Overall, the study aims to support sustainable construction in Bangladesh.

Research Methodology

This study is based on secondary data. No primary survey was conducted in this research. No direct interviews or field investigations were carried out. The study mainly relies on published and available information. Secondary sources provided the necessary data for this research including journal articles, conference papers, research reports, books, and credible online publications. Official reports and institutional documents were also reviewed where relevant. The selected data were related to green building practices in Bangladesh and other relevant contexts. The research adopted a descriptive as well as analytical methodology. The collected information was reviewed and organized systematically. The study examined the current status of green buildings in Bangladesh. It also analyzed the key benefits of green building practices. Major barriers to wider adoption were also identified. In addition, future prospects and possible opportunities were explored. The collected information was grouped into several themes. These themes include present condition, environmental and economic benefits, implementation challenges, and future development opportunities. The findings were then interpreted based on the objectives of the study. This study has some limitations. Since it is based on secondary data, the findings depend on the quality and availability of published sources. Some recent or unpublished information may not be included. The absence of primary data may also limit the depth of analysis. However, the study still provides a useful overview of the current status and future prospects of green buildings in Bangladesh.
